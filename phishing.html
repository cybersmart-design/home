📋 Phishing Detection Guide

alternate_email1. Examine the Sender
Check the full email address, not just the display name. Phishers use addresses like support@paypa1.com instead of paypal.com.
chevron_rightLook for typos in domain names (rnicrosoft vs microsoft)
chevron_rightUnexpected sender? Be cautious even if name looks familiar
chevron_rightFree email services (Gmail, Yahoo) used for official company comms = red flag
link2. Inspect Links Carefully
Hover without clicking to see the real URL. Phishing links mimic legitimate domains with small changes.
chevron_rightIP addresses (192.168.x.x) instead of domain names = suspicious
chevron_rightShortened URLs (bit.ly, tinyurl) hide the real destination
chevron_rightMisspellings in links: go0gle.com, amaozn.com
warning3. Content Red Flags
Urgency or threats - "Your account will be locked!" creates panic to bypass critical thinking.
chevron_rightPoor spelling/grammar - legitimate companies proofread
chevron_rightGeneric greetings ("Dear Customer") instead of your name
chevron_rightRequests for passwords, gift cards, or personal info
chevron_rightSuspicious attachments (.exe, .zip, .html)
verified4. Verify Independently
Contact the company directly using official channels (website, phone number), NOT the contact info in the suspicious message.
psychology5. Social Engineering Red Flags
chevron_rightCEO asking for gift cards via email = 100% scam
chevron_rightUnexpected invoices or legal threats
chevron_rightRequests for secrecy or "urgent action required"
