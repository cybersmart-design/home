Types of Risks & Threats

Explore key cybersecurity risks and learn how to protect yourself.

Cybersecurity is not just about technology — it's about understanding the risks that exist in the digital world. Below are some of the most common risks and threats you may encounter online. Click or tap each risk to learn both the threat and how to respond.

Malware

Malware is software designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems, such as viruses, worms, and ransomware.
Solution: Keep your devices updated, install antivirus software, avoid suspicious downloads, and back up your data regularly.

Phishing

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card details.
Solution: Verify email and website legitimacy, never click on suspicious links, and enable two-factor authentication.

Data Breach

A data breach occurs when confidential information is accessed or stolen by unauthorized users, compromising privacy and security.
Solution: Use strong passwords, enable multi-factor authentication, and monitor accounts for unusual activity.

Social Engineering

Social engineering uses deception to manipulate individuals into performing actions or divulging confidential information.
Solution: Verify identities, question unusual requests, and never share passwords or personal info unexpectedly.

Denial of Service (DoS)

A DoS attack aims to make a network or service unavailable by overwhelming it with traffic.
Solution: Use network firewalls, DDoS protection services, and monitor traffic for unusual activity.
