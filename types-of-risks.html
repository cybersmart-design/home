Threat 01

Malware

Short for "malicious software," malware is any program designed to infiltrate, damage, or exploit computer systems. This umbrella category includes viruses, worms, trojans, spyware, and ransomware. Malware can steal sensitive data, corrupt files, hijack system resources for cryptomining, or provide attackers with persistent remote access through backdoors.

Over 1 billion malware programs currently exist worldwide

Threat 02

Phishing

Phishing attacks use deceptive emails, messages, or websites to trick victims into revealing sensitive information such as login credentials, credit card numbers, or personal data. Modern phishing campaigns are increasingly sophisticated, using AI-generated content and pixel-perfect replicas of legitimate websites. Spear phishing targets specific individuals with personalized messages for higher success rates.

60% of all breaches begin with phishing (ENISA 2024)

Threat 03

Ransomware

Ransomware encrypts victims' files and demands payment — typically in cryptocurrency — for the decryption key. Modern ransomware operators employ "double extortion," stealing data before encryption and threatening to leak it publicly. Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) has industrialized these attacks, allowing even low-skill criminals to deploy sophisticated ransomware with profit-sharing models.

300% increase in ransomware incidents since 2019

Threat 04

Data Breach

A data breach occurs when sensitive, protected, or confidential information is accessed, copied, or transmitted by an unauthorized party. Breaches can result from external attacks, insider threats, or misconfigured cloud services. In 2024, Verizon's DBIR analyzed over 22,000 security incidents and 12,000 confirmed breaches. The consequences include financial loss, regulatory penalties, and irreparable reputational damage.

100B+ stolen credential records circulated on darknet in 2024

Threat 05

Social Engineering

Social engineering manipulates human psychology to bypass technical security controls. Attackers exploit trust, urgency, authority, or fear to trick victims into divulging confidential information or performing actions that compromise security. Techniques include pretexting, baiting, tailgating, and vishing (voice phishing). AI voice synthesis tools like ElevenLabs now enable convincing deepfake phone calls.

98% of cyber attacks rely on social engineering tactics
Identity Verification Employee Training Zero Trust Policies

Threat 06

Denial-of-Service (DoS/DDoS)

Denial-of-Service attacks overwhelm a target system with a flood of traffic, rendering it unavailable to legitimate users. Distributed DoS (DDoS) attacks leverage botnets — armies of thousands of compromised devices — to generate massive traffic volumes simultaneously. These attacks target businesses, government services, and critical infrastructure, often as a smokescreen for other intrusions happening concurrently.

IoT botnets enable attacks exceeding 1 Tbps of traffic

Threat 07

Zero-Day Exploits

Zero-day exploits target previously unknown vulnerabilities in software, operating systems, or hardware — before developers have had any time ("zero days") to release a patch. These are among the most dangerous threats because no specific defense exists yet. In 2024, over 40,000 new vulnerabilities were added to the National Vulnerability Database, a 39% increase over 2023. Attackers actively trade zero-day exploits on darknet forums.
